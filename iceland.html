Iceland

Iceland is known as a geothermal wonder, with its hot springs and geysers. The country is beautiful to visit year-round; however, your experiences may be drastically different depending on what time of year you visit. For example, when visiting over the summer, you could experience days with up to 21 hours of sunlight, versus a winter visit where you could only experience up to 5 hours of sunlight on any given day. Visiting in the winter with the lack of sunlight contributes to it also being known as a great place to see the Northern Lights.

Reykjavik is its capital city and where many of its attractions originate. Within Reykjavik, it would be worthwhile to sign up for a walking tour to explore the city. Another option for exploring further outside of the city is to sign up for a bus tour to adventure out to more of the geothermal wonders.

Alex's Atlas Travel Insights:
Dublin, Ireland is only a 2.5 hour flight from Reykjavik, Iceland. If you are looking to explore more of the world during your trip, consider adding a trip to Ireland to your itinerary.

Glacier HikeTo Top

If you have ever wanted to hike on a glacier, Iceland is the place to do it. A Glacier Tour is one of the most exciting activities. This is a guided tour where safety equipment (e.g., crampons for your boots) is provided. Even when visiting in the summer, it is recommended to bring warm attire for the glacier. If you are lucky, you may even have an opportuity to drink fresh water while on your hike.

Alex's Atlas Travel Insights:
If you enjoy hiking and are looking for a new hiking challenge to look forward to, consider visiting Mount Fuji in Japan.

Gullfoss WaterfallTo Top

The Gullfoss Waterfall, also known as the "Golden Falls", provides an awe-inspiring landscape. It is one of the main attractions on any Golden Circle tour offered in Iceland.

Black Sand BeachTo Top

The Reynisfjara Black Sand Beach is a magnificent sight to behold. Its black sand was formed from hot lava. The beach is found in Iceland's South Coast and can be visited as part of most bus tours that highlight Iceland's beautiful South Coast. Another geological wonder that can be spotted at the beach is its towering basalt columns.

Golden CircleTo Top

Iceland's Golden Circle is a compilation of a few major attractions in Iceland. This includes Thingvellir National Park, The Geysir Geothermal Area, and Gullfoss Waterfall (mentioned above). Together, these attractions provide insight into Iceland's geological and geothermal history. The Golden Circle can easily be visited as part of a guided bus tour or visited by car.

Hallgrímskirkja ChurchTo Top

When visiting Reykjavik in Iceland, it would be nearly impossible to miss the Hallgrímskirkja Church. It is the second tallest building in all of Iceland. Beyond that, its architecture is very unique with its curved spire that somewhat resembles the basalt columns you may see at the Black Sand Beach.

Alex's Atlas Travel Insights:
If you are looking to visit other iconic churches, consider a trip to France where you can see Notre Dame and the Basilique du Sacré Coeur.
