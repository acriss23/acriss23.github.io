Japan

Japan is a beautiful island country off the coast of East Asia. Many visitors travel to Japan in the spring, around March and April, to experienc cherry blossoms in bloom. For more adventurous travelers, July and August are good times to visit to be able to hike to the top of Mount Fuji.

Japan is also home to the most populated city in the world - Tokyo. With such a large population, it is no surprise that Japan has a great metro system, including the well-known bullet train (Shinkansen) that can travel up to almost 200mph. As a tourist in Japan, an efficient and cost-effective way to utilize the railways is through the advanced purchase of the Japan Rail Pass (JR Pass).

Arashiyama Bamboo GroveTo Top

As the name suggests, the Arashiyama Bamboo Grove is a forest with towering bamboo overhead. You can walk through the paths of the forest to take in the gentle sounds of the bamboo swaying in the wind. To visit the bamboo grove, you can take the JR line from Kyoto Station to Saga-Arashiyama Station. From there, it is a quaint 15 minute stroll through the town to get to the entrance of the bamboo grove.

Ghibli MuseumTo Top

Ghibli Museum in Mitaka is a museum centered around the works of Hayao Miyazaki, who animated a number of films through his animation studio, Studio Ghibli. Some of these films include "My Neighbor Totoro," "Kiki's Delivery Service," and "Spirited Away." This is a must-see for any fans of Studio Ghibli films, where you can learn about the film animation process, see some of Miyazaki's sketches, and more. Note that Ghibli Museum tickets must be reserved in advance.

KinkakujiTo Top

Kinkaku-ji is a Buddhist temple and World Heritage site in Kyoto. In English, it is known as the "Golden Pavilion" due to the gold-leaf coating on the building. While the temple dates back to the 1300's, the current building was reconstructed in 1955 after a fire occured at the property. While it is breathtaking to see in person, there is also an option to do a virtual Kinkaku-ji tour.

Mount FujiTo Top

Mount Fuji is the tallest peak in Japan, at 3,776 meters. It is only open to hikers in July and August due to dangerous climbing conditions outside of that timeframe. You can check the Official Web Site for Mount Fuji Climbing for advisories and other information before you take off on your trip. Some hikers choose to climb to the top and back down in one day, while others may reserve space at the lodge near the top of the mountain to rest until just before sunrise. A couple hours before sunrise, they pack up and hike the rest of the way to the peak to see the sunrise from the top of Mount Fuji.

Kamakura DaibutsuTo Top

Kamakura Daibutsu, or "The Great Buddha of Kamakura," is a large, bronze statue of Amida Buddha that was created in 1252. Including its base, it stands at over 13 meters tall. The statue is hollow, so it is possible to line up behind the statue for a chance to see its interior.
