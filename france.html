France

France is known for its art, fashion, food, wine, and many identifiable monuments. Many memorable activities can be done in its capital city of Paris. I visited during the month of July and the weather at that time was perfect - around 70° during the day. Signing up for a walking tour is an efficient way to explore the city on foot with an experienced guide. Many of these walking tours should be reserved in advance and are free with a recommended donation to your guide.

The Eiffel TowerTo Top

The Eiffel Tower may be one of the first monuments you envision when someone mentions France. If you are staying within Paris, it shouldn't be a challenge to spot it. Be sure to look out for it at night as it lights up the city. In the evening, it also presents a sparkling light show for five minutes at the top of each hour. If the timing aligns for you, you may also be lucky to catch fireworks on Bastille Day, July 14. It is also worth it to buy an Eiffel Tower ticket to ride the elevator to the top, where you can get breathtaking views of the city.

The Louvre MuseumTo Top

The Louvre Museum in Paris houses many of the world's most famous pieces of art, including the Mona Lisa. The building itself is also an artistic landmark itself and has made appearances in a number of films. Bastille Day, July 14, is a great day to visit as the museum is open and free of charge due to the national holiday.

Palace of VersaillesTo Top

The Palace of Versailles is another French landmark. Formerly the residence of King Louis XIII, it was constructed in the 1600's. It was during the 1800's that it became a museum, where visitors can explore the palace and its surrounding estate, including the garden with its miraculous fountains and shrubbery. The palace itself is now a museum that houses thousands of pieces of art work. Purchasing a Palace of Versailles ticket would allow you to see how royalty lived in the 1600's in France.

Notre DameTo Top

Notre Dame is an active Catholic cathedral in Paris that was constructed in 1345. Like the Eiffel Tower, it is a very recognizable landmark in Paris, with appearances in a number of films. Unfortunately, the cathedral caught fire in 2019 and is undergoing reconstruction that is not expected to be completed until after 2024. When it reopens to the public, it would be worthwhile to visit and check out available times to climb the towers.

Basilique du Sacré CoeurTo Top

The Basilique du Sacré Coeur is an active Roman Catholic church in Paris. It exhibits stunning architecture and stained glass windows. It is located at the highest point in the city at Montmartre, and as such, is also known as a good vantage point to view the city.
