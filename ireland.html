Ireland

Ireland, also known as the "Emerald Isle", is a beautiful country to visit. Among all of the greenery, you will also find a number of castles. Starting in the capital city of Dublin, you could rent a car to take a road trip out to many of the castles. Some of them include: Kilkenny Castle, Cahir Castle, and Blarney Castle (home of the famous Blarney Stone). If you're from the USA like me, be careful if you rent a car - you would need to drive on the left side of the road and some of the country roads can be extremely narrow. If you are concerned about driving, bus tours may be a safer (and more convenient) route.

Alex's Atlas Travel Insights:
Reykjavik, Iceland is only a 2.5 hour flight from Dublin, Ireland. If you are looking to explore more of the world during your trip, consider adding a trip to Iceland to your itinerary.

Cliffs of MoherTo Top

The Cliffs of Moher is a must-see landscape in Ireland. The cliffs, which branch out into the sea, are contrasted with the surrounding farm land. Like many other landmarks, the Cliffs can also be seen in films like "The Princess Bride" and "Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince." While it may take a few hours to drive from Dublin out to the cliffs, it is well worth it. You may be able to find a bus tour that will guide the way for you, such as Paddywagon's Cliffs of Moher Tour.

Giant's CausewayTo Top

Giant's Causeway is a geological wonder in Northern Ireland, and as such, is also a UNESCO world heritage site. It comprises thousands of basalt columns formed as a result of volcanic eruptions. This site can be visited as part of a bus tour that starts in Dublin, Ireland, such as Finn McCools' Giant's Causeway Tour.

Ring of KerryTo Top

The Ring of Kerry is a scenic drive that takes you through beautiful pastures and villages to get a sense of what Ireland's countryside is like. A typical bus tour, such as Paddywagon's Ring of Kerry Tour, will take you from Cork, to Killarney, Killorglin, Dingle Bay, Waterville, Sneem, Killarney National Park, and Torc Waterfall.

Guinness StorehouseTo Top

At the Guinness Storehouse in Dublin, you can learn about the history of Guinness in Ireland. You can tour the brewery, learn to pour the perfect pint, and relax on the fourth floor of the Guinness Storehouse.

Kilmainham GaolTo Top

Kilmainham Gaol is a former prison turned museum in Dublin. It was active from 1796 through 1924. The building is eerie to witness in person through a guided tour; however, it is a great opportunity to learn about the history in Ireland and the many war prisoners who were detained during its operation. The museum provides many educational opportunities, including providing an online Kilmainham Gaol tour if you prefer to experience it that way.
